    If RP2040 hardware is available:
    1. Flash the .uf2 to the Pico: copy firmware/.pio/build/rpico_rp2040_standalone/*.uf2 to the
       RP2040 mass-storage device (hold BOOTSEL while connecting USB, then copy).
    2. Open a serial terminal at 115200 baud on the USB CDC port.
    3. Confirm boot: you should see `[biba]` log lines within 2 seconds.
    4. With ELRS transmitter OFF: confirm `fs=1 arm=0` in log (failsafe active, disarmed).
    5. Turn transmitter ON: confirm `fs=0` in log within 500ms (CRSF lock-in).
    6. Arm (CH5 high): confirm `ARMED` log line; motor enable and SSR should activate.
    7. Apply throttle slowly: confirm motors spin and `L=` / `R=` values in log ramp up gradually.
    8. Release throttle: confirm values ramp back to 0, no abrupt jump.
    9. Disarm: confirm `DISARMED` log line; motors stop.
    10. Turn transmitter OFF while armed: confirm `FAILSAFE` within 500ms, motors stop.

    If hardware is NOT available:
    1. Confirm test suite is green (Task 1 output).
    2. Confirm .uf2 artifact exists (Task 2 output).
    3. Mark as approved — hardware test deferred to first field session.
  </how-to-verify>
